## Accuracy Review

### ✅ Strengths

1. **Historical Information - ACCURATE**
   - H.C. Sorby (1826-1908) correctly identified as the "Father of Metallography"
   - 1863 date for Sorby's work is historically accurate
   - Description of Sorby's contributions (adapting petrography techniques, developing sample preparation methods) is correct

2. **Technical Definitions - MOSTLY ACCURATE**
   - Microstructure definitions are correct
   - Phase descriptions (ferrite, austenite, martensite, pearlite, bainite) are accurate
   - Sample preparation steps are correctly described
   - ASTM standards information is accurate (Committee E-4 formed in 1916)

3. **Process Descriptions - ACCURATE**
   - The five-step preparation process (sectioning, mounting, grinding, polishing, etching) is correctly outlined
   - Common problems and solutions table is practical and accurate
   - Safety information is appropriate

### ⚠️ Issues Found

1. **CRITICAL: Cementite Appearance - INACCURATE**
   - **Location:** Line 451
   - **Issue:** States "Appears dark in etched microstructures"
   - **Problem:** This is too general and depends on the etchant used:
     - With **nital**: Cementite typically appears dark
     - With **picral**: Cementite appears light/white
   - **Impact:** Could mislead readers about cementite identification
   - **Recommendation:** Change to "Appears dark with nital etching, light with picral etching. Appearance depends on the etchant used."

2. **MINOR: Glossary Inconsistency**
   - **Location:** Glossary definition vs. guide content
   - **Issue:** Glossary says cementite appears "white or light-colored" while guide says "dark"
   - **Recommendation:** Both should be updated to note etchant dependency

3. **MINOR: Phase Diagram Mention**
   - **Location:** Line 512-518
   - **Issue:** Mentions phase diagrams but doesn't explain their practical use or provide examples
   - **Recommendation:** Either expand with a brief example or add a link to a more detailed resource
